20 years from now...

First there was nothing...

...Then there was everything

I can smell The City that lies just over the horizon. It smells like lost children that have, after centuries, forgotten that they are indeed lost. They've instead come to believe that the path which they are on is the way home and that the tongue that they learned from wolves is their native tongue.

I've been wandering this desert of humanity for so long that I myself have forgotten my own name. I feel as if now I simply wander in search of it. In search of who I am. Who we are. Who are we?

As I near the horizon I can see the smoke and soot rising from the great City and I'm reminded of the fateful words that were once written about this place.

"They said to each other, 'Come, let's make bricks and bake them thoroughly.' They used brick instead of stone, and tar for mortar. Then they said, 'Come, let us build ourselves a city, with a tower that reaches to the heavens, so that we may make a name for ourselves; otherwise we will be scattered over the face of the whole earth.'"

"But the Lord came down to see the city and the tower the people were building. The Lord said, 'If as one people speaking the same language they have begun to do this, then nothing they plan to do will be impossible for them. Come, let us go down and confuse their language so they will not understand each other.'"

"So the Lord scattered them from there over all the earth, and they stopped building the city. That is why it was called Babel, because there the Lord confused the language of the whole world. From there the Lord scattered them over the face of the whole earth."
